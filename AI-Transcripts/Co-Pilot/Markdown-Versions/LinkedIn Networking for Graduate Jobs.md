### 🚀 Step-by-Step LinkedIn Outreach Strategy

**1. Identify people you genuinely find interesting**
- Target alumni from University of Bradford, guest speakers you’ve hosted through PiSoc, or professionals in fields you’re curious about (automation, software engineering, consulting, etc.).
- Use LinkedIn filters: alumni search, “Computer Science” + “Bradford,” or companies you admire.

**2. Make a *small ask* (low effort, high relevance)**
- Instead of “Can you help me get a job?” ask something like:
  - “I saw you worked on [topic/project]. Could you point me to an article, paper, or resource you found insightful when learning about it?”
  - “I’m exploring [field]. Is there a book, blog, or talk you’d recommend as a good starting point?”
- This shows respect for their time and makes it easy for them to respond.

**3. Read and study what they share**
- Actually engage with the resource. Take notes, form an opinion, and think about how it connects to your interests.
- This is where you stand out: most people don’t follow through. You will.

**4. Circle back with gratitude + insight**
- Message them again: “Thanks for recommending [resource]. I found the section on [specific insight] especially useful. It made me think about [your reflection]. Would you be open to a quick call to discuss how you applied this in your career?”
- This positions you as thoughtful, proactive, and worth investing time in.

---

### ✨ Why this works for you
- You already have visibility on LinkedIn, so people are more likely to respond positively.
- Your PiSoc leadership and event management experience give you natural conversation starters — you can reference shared experiences or mutual connections.
- You’re not asking for a job directly; you’re building trust and credibility, which often leads to referrals or opportunities organically.

### 📝 Outreach Message Template (polished but warm)

```
Thanks so much,
Hasan
```

Then follow up later with your reflections and a gentle ask for a call.
